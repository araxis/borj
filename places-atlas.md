# Shahnameh Places and Fortress Atlas Ledger

Image: ![Places atlas](../../src/assets/atlases/places-atlas.png)

Grid: 5 columns x 6 rows. Each image cell follows the exact position below.

Source note: story references name the Ferdowsi Shahnameh episode/cycle used for the short and detailed descriptions. Rows marked "game site based on" are game-built locations derived from a Shahnameh place, object, or episode rather than exact named buildings in the poem.

| Position | Place / Site | Ferdowsi Shahnameh Story Reference | Short Description | Shahnameh-Based Description |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Row 1, Col 1 | Zabulistan Champion Keep / دژ پهلوانی زابلستان | Sistani heroic cycle: Sam, Zal, Rostam, and the House of Nariman | A game fortress based on Rostam's heroic homeland. | Zabulistan is the epic base of the Sistani champions, especially Sam, Zal, and Rostam. This keep is a game site based on that homeland: mountain defenses, pahlavan banners, and the military gravity of Rostam's house. |
| Row 1, Col 2 | Sistan Reedland Fortress / دژ نیزار سیستان | Sistani heroic cycle; Rostam's homeland and road to Zabulistan | A defensive wetland stronghold for the Sistani line. | Sistan should be represented as a hard frontier landscape supporting the Sistani hero-family. The fortress is a game-built site grounded in the Sistan/Zabulistan world: reeds, water channels, watchtowers, and rugged local defense. |
| Row 1, Col 3 | Kabul Palace / کاخ کابل | Zal and Rudabeh; Birth of Rostam | Rudabeh's palace and the setting of the Zal-Rudabeh romance. | Kabul belongs directly to the Zal and Rudabeh story through Rudabeh, daughter of Mehrab. The palace should reference the balcony, long hair, secret courtship, and the chain of events that leads to Rostam's birth. |
| Row 1, Col 4 | Balkh Royal City / شهر شاهی بلخ | Lohrasp and Goshtasp cycle; Arjasp's attacks in the Kayanian/Sasanian-transition material | A major royal city tied to later Kayanian power. | Balkh should be treated as an old royal and strategic city in the later Shahnameh world. Its atlas entry should emphasize gates, courtly power, and vulnerability to the wars around Goshtasp and Arjasp. |
| Row 1, Col 5 | Mazandaran / مازندران | Kay Kavus's expedition to Mazandaran; Rostam's Seven Labours | The dangerous land of divs that traps Kay Kavus. | Mazandaran is the setting of Kay Kavus's disastrous ambition and Rostam's rescue mission. Its image should show forest, cave, fog, and div-haunted terrain because the Seven Labours unfold through this hostile world. |
| Row 2, Col 1 | Alborz Mountain Realm / البرز | Birth of Zal; Simurgh raises Zal on Mount Alborz | The high mountain realm of Simurgh's nest. | Alborz is a place, not a character. Its Shahnameh importance comes from Zal's exposure and rescue by Simurgh, so the image should show unreachable height, a vast nest, white feathers, and sacred distance from ordinary courts. |
| Row 2, Col 2 | Mount Damavand / دماوند | Fereydun binds Zahhak on Mount Damavand | The mountain prison of Zahhak. | Damavand is a place and symbol of restrained tyranny. Its story reference is Fereydun's victory over Zahhak, after which Zahhak is bound in the mountain instead of simply killed. The image should show chains, snow, stone, and a buried threat. |
| Row 2, Col 3 | Samangan Palace / کاخ سمنگان | Rostam and Sohrab: Rostam in Samangan; Tahmineh and the birth of Sohrab | The palace where Tahmineh meets Rostam and Sohrab's tragedy begins. | Samangan is the origin point of the Rostam-Sohrab tragedy. Its design should show royal warmth and night-court intimacy, but also the shadow of the token that is meant to identify Sohrab and fails to prevent disaster. |
| Row 2, Col 4 | Estakhr / استخر | Sasanian royal geography; Ardashir and later kings | A stone royal center of the Sasanian world. | Estakhr belongs to the later historical-royal geography of the Shahnameh. Its atlas treatment should feel older and more ceremonial than a simple fort: stone terraces, columns, and a sense of dynastic continuity. |
| Row 2, Col 5 | Madayen / Ctesiphon / مداین / تیسفون | Sasanian section: Khosrow Anushirvan, Hormozd, Khosrow Parviz | The imperial Sasanian capital setting. | Madayen/Ctesiphon belongs to the late royal world of the Shahnameh. It should be shown with a vast arch, river, court ceremony, and imperial scale, connecting the game to the poem's historical Sasanian arc. |
| Row 3, Col 1 | Turan War-Court City / شهر دربار توران | Siyavash in Turan; Afrasiab; Kay Khosrow's war against Afrasiab | A rival royal court, not an uncivilized wasteland. | Turan is the opposing political world of Afrasiab, Piran, Farangis, and Kay Khosrow's birth. The city should show court culture, cavalry power, and royal tension so the opposition remains human and epic rather than crude. |
| Row 3, Col 2 | Makran Frontier / مرز مکران | Later campaign geography in the Shahnameh's royal sections | A harsh frontier at the edge of campaign routes. | Makran functions as a distant campaign frontier in the epic geography. This game site should emphasize heat, rock, wind, and logistical difficulty instead of treating it as a fantasy monster land. |
| Row 3, Col 3 | Dez-i Sepid / White Fortress / دژ سپید | Rostam and Sohrab: Hojir and Gordafarid defend the White Fortress | The fortress where Sohrab first meets Iran's defenders. | Dez-i Sepid is directly tied to the Rostam and Sohrab tragedy. Hojir guards it, Gordafarid rides out from it, and Sohrab's attack sets the recognition disaster in motion. The image should show white walls, a high gate, spear defense, and heroic tension. |
| Row 3, Col 4 | Dez-i Roein / Iron Fortress / دژ رویین | Shahnameh fortress motif; game site based on iron/bronze invulnerability imagery | A game fortress based on iron-hard defensive imagery. | This is a game-built fortress concept tied to the Shahnameh's recurring language of hard metal, invulnerable bodies, and difficult citadels rather than a single full episode in this atlas list. It should be visually honest as an iron-walled military site. |
| Row 3, Col 5 | Dez-i Bahman / دژ بهمن | Named fortress tradition; game site based on Kayanian royal-fortress motifs | A game fortress based on Kayanian royal defense. | Dez-i Bahman is used here as a game site connected to Kayanian fortress imagery and royal defense rather than a full standalone episode in the ledger. The image should show cold stone, banners, and dynastic weight. |
| Row 4, Col 1 | Gang-Dez / گنگ‌دژ | Kay Khosrow and Afrasiab cycle; legendary fortress-city of Turan | A legendary fortress-city tied to the far Turanian world. | Gang-Dez should feel distant, fortified, and almost unreachable. Its reference belongs to the Afrasiab/Kay Khosrow geography of pursuit and royal opposition, so the design should convey both wonder and danger. |
| Row 4, Col 2 | Alanan Fortress / دژ آلانان | Shahnameh frontier geography; game site based on northern/frontier war routes | A frontier high-pass stronghold. | Alanan is treated here as a frontier-derived game fortress rather than a major independent narrative episode. The design should show mountain passes, watchfires, and the sense of guarding a difficult border. |
| Row 4, Col 3 | Gonbadan Castle / قلعه گنبدان | Shahnameh named-place tradition; game site based on domed royal strongholds | A domed stronghold for court and defense. | Gonbadan is handled as a game location inspired by named-place and domed-castle imagery in the epic world. It should read as architectural culture, not a generic fort: domes, walls, and a guarded approach. |
| Row 4, Col 4 | Simurgh Nest Tower / برج آشیان سیمرغ | Birth of Zal; Birth of Rostam; Rostam and Esfandiyar | A game tower based on Simurgh's mountain nest. | This is not Simurgh herself but an architectural echo of her Shahnameh role. The tower should reference the Alborz nest, healing feathers, guidance at Rostam's birth, and counsel in the Esfandiyar crisis. |
| Row 4, Col 5 | Kaveh Rebellion Forge / آهنگری شورش کاوه | Zahhak, Kaveh, and Fereydun | A forge-site based on Kaveh raising revolt. | The forge should come from Kaveh's Shahnameh moment: a blacksmith turns grief and anger into public resistance, and his leather apron becomes the Derafsh-e Kaviani. Sparks, anvils, workers, and the rising banner are essential. |
| Row 5, Col 1 | Fereydun Mace Hall / تالار گرز فریدون | Zahhak and Fereydun; Fereydun's ox-headed mace | A ceremonial hall for Fereydun's victory over Zahhak. | This game site is based on Fereydun's defeat of Zahhak with the ox-headed mace and the restoration of justice. The hall should carry broken chains, the mace image, and the solemn duty of kingship after victory. |
| Row 5, Col 2 | Siyavash Trial Gate / دروازه آزمون سیاوش | Siyavash and Sudabeh; Siyavash's trial by fire | A gate based on Siyavash's passage through fire. | The gate should reference Siyavash's fire ordeal, where he proves innocence against Sudabeh's accusation. It should feel beautiful and dangerous: ritual flame, a narrow path, a black horse, and tragic purity. |
| Row 5, Col 3 | Key Khosrow Command Hall / تالار فرمان کیخسرو | Giv finds Kay Khosrow; Kay Khosrow's war against Afrasiab; Kay Khosrow's departure | A royal command hall for visionary judgment. | Key Khosrow's hall should draw from his discovery, rightful kingship, revenge for Siyavash, and eventual spiritual departure. It should show order, counsel, war maps, and a light suggesting insight beyond ordinary rule. |
| Row 5, Col 4 | Esfandiyar Brazen Bastion / باروی رویین اسفندیار | Rostam and Esfandiyar | A defensive bastion based on Esfandiyar's near-invulnerable body. | This is a game site based on Esfandiyar's brazen-body motif and his fatal conflict with Rostam. The building should carry bronze/iron surfaces, arrow slits, and the tension between sacred duty and doomed command. |
| Row 5, Col 5 | Gordafarid Spear Fort / دژ نیزه گردآفرید | Rostam and Sohrab: Gordafarid at Dez-i Sepid | A fort based on Gordafarid's defense against Sohrab. | Gordafarid's site should connect directly to the White Fortress episode. The architecture should emphasize a woman warrior's active defense: spear racks, high gate, riding path, and watchful courage under siege. |
| Row 6, Col 1 | Manijeh Garden / باغ منیژه | Bijan and Manijeh | A hidden garden based on Manijeh protecting Bijan. | Manijeh's garden should reference the Bijan and Manijeh story, especially her loyalty after Bijan is imprisoned. It should show night, secrecy, a route toward the pit, and the risk of compassion across enemy lines. |
| Row 6, Col 2 | Rakhsh Black Stable Fort / دژ اصطبل رخش مشکی | Rostam chooses Rakhsh; Rostam's Seven Labours; Rostam and Shaghad | A military stable-fort for Rostam's black warhorse. | Rakhsh should be treated as a heroic companion, so this fort is more than a stable. It should refer to Rostam choosing the unmatched horse, Rakhsh saving him in the Seven Labours, and both dying in Shaghad's trap. |
| Row 6, Col 3 | Derafsh Standard Hall / تالار درفش | Zahhak, Kaveh, and Fereydun; Kaveh's apron becomes the Derafsh-e Kaviani | A banner hall based on Kaveh's rebel standard. | The Derafsh hall should translate Kaveh's apron-standard into architecture: a place where revolt becomes shared identity. It should show banners, gathered people, forge-light, and the movement from private grief to national symbol. |
| Row 6, Col 4 | Babr-e Bayan Armor Ward / جایگاه ببر بیان | Rostam cycle; Rostam's armor/garment Babr-e Bayan | A relic ward based on Rostam's protective battle gear. | Babr-e Bayan belongs to Rostam's heroic identity across his battles. The ward is a game site for that relic: armor display, animal-skin patterning, shields, and the idea of protection earned through trial. |
| Row 6, Col 5 | Arash Horizon Watch / دیدبان افق آرش | Manuchehr-Afrasiyab border tradition; allusive/traditional rather than a full Ferdowsi episode | A watchtower for the arrow that defines the border. | Arash is a central Iranian border legend but not a full narrative episode in Ferdowsi's Shahnameh like Rostam's stories. The watchtower should honestly frame him as an allusive/traditional figure: a vast horizon, a bow platform, and the line between Iran and Turan. |
